Call or click before you dig – it’s the law

If renovations are on the agenda this spring, be sure to put safety ﬁrst.

At least ﬁve business days before the ﬁrst shovel breaks ground, contact Ontario One Call to locate and mark utility lines on your property. They’re fast, their service is free and they’ll save you from costly – possibly dangerous – accidents. Here’s what you need to know:

  1. It’s against the law in Ontario to dig without having natural gas lines located.
  2. Digging or excavating could damage the network of hydro, gas, water and TV/telephone lines that may lie just below the surface of your property.
  3. Damage to these pipes or wires could create a safety hazard or shut down service to an entire neighbourhood until the system is repaired.
  4. Business owners or their contractors are liable for damages, which could include a costly lawsuit if personal injury or property damage result.
  5. Contact Ontario One Call at on1call.com or toll free at 1-800-400-2255.
  6. You’ll be provided with a free locate report of the underground utility lines on your property so you can complete your project safely and responsibly.
  7. Visit uniongas.com/safety for more information about natural gas safety.

An efficient account manager, at your fingertips

MyAccount is designed to save you both time and effort. Your secure online account lets you:

  • Access 24 months of billing history • Pay your bills
  • Update address, contact or payment information – no need to call
  • Track and compare up to 24 months of energy use – helpful for managing cash flow

For even more convenience and less paperwork, sign up for Paperless Billing.

  • Get your bill emailed to you as a PDF – no login or password needed
  • Opt for an annual statement of gas use and payments every January – useful for tax returns
  • Choose to receive payment reminders by email so you never forget to pay

To use MyAccount

  1. Go to uniongas.com/myaccount on your smartphone or computer.
  2. Click on Login or Register Now (You’ll need your 14-digit Union Gas account number to register).

Cap-and-trade recap

  • The Ontario government’s cap-and-trade program began Jan. 1, 2017, and the costs are included in the Delivery line of your natural gas bill
  • Use the calculator at uniongas.com/capandtrade to estimate your monthly cap-and-trade charge and its impact on your bottom line
  • Visit uniongas.com/capandtrade for programs and tips to reduce your gas use and the cost of cap and trade
